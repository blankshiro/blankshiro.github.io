# Domain 1: Security and Risk Management
1. Concerned about changes in technology may make content outdated? Use **content reviews**.
2. Identify remaining level of risk to the organization after adopting security controls? **Residual risk**.
3. User is storing information on her service that violates the third party’s copyright? **DMCA**.
4. GDPR? **The right to be forgotten**.
5. Purchasing insurance is a means of **transferring risk**.
6. Which one of the following elements of information is not considered PII? **Student Identification Number**.
7. What rule requires that senior executives take personal responsibility for infosec matters? **Prudent man rule**.
8. In violation of Canon IV of the Code of Ethics: “Advance and protect the profession.”? Any certified or licensed professional may bring charges.
9. Facilitate exchange of customer info? Standard contractual clauses.
10. Privacy of customer's financial info? **GLBA**.
11. Involving a government contractor? **FISMA**.
12. Compliance with export control laws? Encryption software.
13. Exploit vulnerability with admin rights? Elevation of privilege.
14. Business continuity effort and accept risk? Document your decision-making process.
15. Which control categories describe a fence around a facility? Physical, Deterrent and Preventive.
16. Developing a business continuity plan? Need combine both quantitative and qualitative risk assessment.
17. Stealing trade secrets from a U.S.corp? Economic Espionage Act.
18. React in a situation using the same level of care that would be expected from any reasonable person? Due care.
19. Protection of intellectual property would be least likely be part of the oraganizational process during an acquisition. 
20. Administrative investigation? There is no standard.
21. Want to license tech to other companies for use but also want to prevent unauthorized use of tech? Patent.
22. Which action might be taken as part of business continuity plan? Implementing RAID (because it provides fault tolerance).
23. After developing a business impact analysis, the team should first create a list of assets and then develop a value for each asset.
24. Implement an IPS to block common network attacks? Risk mitigation (lowering probability or impact of risk).
25. SCA? Government.
26. A case against that individual that will lead to imprisonment? Need proof beyond a reasonable doubt.
27. Trademark protection extends to words and symbols used to represent an organization, product, or service.
28. Ransomware? Availability attack.
29. Not automatically subjected to the privacy and security requirements of HIPAA? Health and fitness app developer.
30. Bombarded with network packets that leads to DoS? Availability principle is violated.
31. **Strategic plans** have a long-term planning horizon of up to **five years** in most cases.
32. Intellectual property protection of trademarks? **USPTO**.
33. Divide critical tasks into discrete components and ensure that no one person has the ability to perform both actions? Separation of duties.
34. Defense contractors? FISMA.
35. Credit card info? PCI DSS.
36. Who implement security controls defined by policy and senior management? Data custodian.
37. Written work or website content stolen? Copyright.
38. Administrative laws that affect business operations? Code of Federal Regulations.
39. Installing a device that blocks attacks is an attempt to **reduce likelihood** of successful attacks.
40. Most effective organizational owner for an infosec program? CIO.
41. Important function do senior managers normally fill on a business continuity planning team? Arbitrating disputes.
